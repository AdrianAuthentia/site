The Role of QA in Application Maintenance

Published on
January 19, 2023
-
3 min read

Quality Assurance (QA) plays a pivotal role in the realm of Application Maintenance and Support (AMS). It's not just about fixing bugs or adding new features; it's about ensuring that these changes don't disrupt the existing functionality or user experience. In fact, according to a report by the Consortium for IT Software Quality, poor software quality costs US organizations approximately $2.08 trillion in 2020[1.

QA in application maintenance involves a range of activities, from regression testing to performance testing. Regression testing ensures that changes or additions haven't broken any existing functionality. Performance testing checks that the application can handle the expected load and responds quickly to user interactions.

Moreover, QA is crucial in ensuring that the application remains secure, especially when changes are made. According to a study by IBM, the average cost of a data breach in 2021 was $4.24 million, the highest in 17 years2. Regular security testing as part of QA can help identify and fix vulnerabilities, protecting both the business and its customers.
