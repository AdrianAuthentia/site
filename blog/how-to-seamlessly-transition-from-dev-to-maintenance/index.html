How to Seamlessly Transition from Dev to Maintenance

Published on
November 3, 2022
-
4 min read

The journey of a digital product doesn't end with its development and launch. In fact, that's just the beginning. The real challenge lies in the transition from active development to the maintenance and enhancement phase, a process that is often overlooked but is crucial for the product's long-term success.

According to a report by the Standish Group, approximately 70% of IT budgets are spent on maintaining and operating existing systems and software1. This statistic underscores the importance of application maintenance and support (AMS) in the software life cycle. However, the transition from development to maintenance can be a complex process, fraught with potential pitfalls.

The first step in this transition is understanding that development and maintenance require different skill sets and mindsets. While development is about creating new features and functionality, maintenance is about ensuring stability, resolving issues, and making incremental improvements. A study by the University of Cambridge found that maintenance activities account for over 60% of all work performed by software engineers2.

The next step is to establish a robust process for handling maintenance tasks. This includes setting up a ticketing system for tracking issues, defining service level agreements (SLAs) for response times, and implementing a system for managing updates and enhancements.
